one red ball, $n$ white balls

1. M: 'Balls of same color'

probab of getting red ball followed by red ball:$ \frac{1}{n+1} \times \frac{1}{n+1}$

probab of getting white ball followed by white ball:$ \frac{n}{n+1} \times \frac{n}{n+1}$

prob(M) = sum of these two.

$$\begin{aligned}P(M) &= \frac{1}{n+1} \times \frac{1}{n+1} + \frac{n}{n+1} \times \frac{n}{n+1} \\ &= \frac{1}{(n+1)^2} + \frac{n^2}{(n+1)^2}\\ &= \frac{n^2+1}{(n+1)^2}\\ \end{aligned}$$

N: 'Balls of different color' $$\begin{aligned}P(N) &= 1- p(M)\\ &= 1-\frac{n^2+1}{(n+1)^2}\\ &= \frac{(n+1)^2-(n^2+1)}{(n+1)^2}\\ &= \frac{n^2 + 2n + 1 - n^2 -1}{(n+1)^2}\\ &= \frac{2n}{(n+1)^2}\\ \end{aligned}$$
